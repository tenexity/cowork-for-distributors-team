# Decision document template

Use this when you're facing a decision that's worth thinking through properly — not every small call, but the ones where getting it wrong costs real margin, a major customer, or a vendor relationship. Examples: dropping a carry line, releasing a big credit hold, opening or closing a branch, firing or hiring a key person, accepting or fighting a major price increase, restructuring rep territories.

---

## The decision

### What's the question?

State the decision as a clear yes/no or either/or question.

**Good:** "Should we drop Brand X as our primary commercial condenser line and move primary allocation to Brand Y?"
**Bad:** "HVAC line review."

### Why does this matter?

What's at stake? What happens if you get it wrong? What's the cost of doing nothing? Two sentences that ground the decision in reality.

### When do you need to decide by?

Is there a hard deadline? A vendor waiting on an answer? A season that forces the call? If no deadline, what's the cost of waiting — does it get harder or easier?

## Analysis

### What would have to be true for each option to be the right call?

- Option A is right if: [condition]
- Option B is right if: [condition]
- Option C is right if: [condition]

### What am I most worried about?

[The risk that keeps you up at night. Name it directly. "We lose the top 3 HVAC contractors if we drop Carrier because they're spec'd into Carrier on all their service contracts."]

### Who's affected and do they know?

Distributor decisions usually affect people outside the room. Name them.

- **Customers:** [Which accounts? How many? Who needs a heads-up?]
- **Vendors:** [Which manufacturers? How and when do we tell them?]
- **Reps:** [Will anyone lose territory value? Who needs to be in the conversation before the decision?]
- **Branches:** [Does this hit branches unevenly? Who needs to prepare?]

### What information would make this decision easier?

What don't you know that you wish you did? Is it worth finding out before deciding, or is that just procrastination?

## How to use this

**Option 1: Fill it in yourself.** Think through on paper, then ask Claude to challenge: "Here's my decision doc. Play the sceptic — what am I missing? Who's not in the room who should be?"

**Option 2: Co-create with Claude.** Describe the decision and ask Claude to walk you through: "I'm thinking about dropping Brand X as our primary line. Help me think this through." Claude will ask questions and build out the analysis.

**Option 3: Use thinking moves for high-stakes calls.** Combine the template with the thinking moves from the Cookbook:
- **Show Your Working** — reason through each option out loud
- **The Sceptic's Chair** — argue against your preferred option
- **Remove the Easy Answer** — take the obvious choice off the table and see what emerges
- **Confidence Check** — after the decision, list every assumption that could be wrong, rate 1-10

## Distributor-specific decision examples

**Carry line decisions:**
"Should we add Brand Z as a secondary water heater line alongside Bradford White?"
"Should we drop the Grundfos pump line after 8 years?"
"Should we expand into commercial refrigeration?"

**Branch decisions:**
"Should we close the Schenectady branch and consolidate into Albany?"
"Should we open a fifth branch in the [city] market?"

**Customer decisions:**
"Should we fire Acme Plumbing after chronic late payments despite high revenue?"
"Should we extend Supercon Mechanical a $500K credit line despite two 90+ day incidents last year?"

**Vendor decisions:**
"Should we accept the Carrier 7% increase as announced, or walk away from primary status?"
"Should we join the Affiliated Distributors buying group?"

**People decisions:**
"Should we replace the Syracuse branch manager after 18 months of missing plan?"
"Should we split the Rochester territory between two reps?"

## Notes

- The "what would have to be true" question is the most powerful part of this template. It turns vague preferences into testable conditions.
- Writing down your gut call first prevents rationalising toward whatever the analysis says. If your conclusion disagrees with your gut, that's worth examining.
- Not every decision needs this template. If you can decide in under 5 minutes, decide. This is for calls where getting it wrong costs more than 5 hours of thinking to get right.
- Save decision docs to the relevant work area, usually `WORK AREAS/[area]/[decision-topic]-decision-project/`. Future you will thank you when a similar decision comes up in three years.
