# Customer QBR one-pager template

Use this for Quarterly Business Reviews with your top accounts. One page. The contractor scans it in 60 seconds; your rep uses it to run a 30-45 minute meeting.

The filled-in example lives in `WORK AREAS/Customer-Accounts/top-25-account-qbr-example-project/outputs/Acme-Plumbing_QBR-One-Pager_v1.md` — read that for reference.

## How to use this

**Data-first workflow:** Pull the day-of ERP data. Paste it into the chat. Say "build a QBR one-pager for [contractor] using our template, meeting date [date]." Claude drafts the facts; you add the flags and talking points.

**Template consistency:** Use the same structure for every account. Contractors who see you with 5 or 10 other distributors will compare, and consistency signals you run a tight ship.

**Trend data matters more than point data.** A 4-week trend on fill rate is what reveals service dips before the contractor asks about them. A point-in-time number can hide a problem.

---

## The rules

- **Day-of data only.** Weekly data ages poorly during peak season. Pull the morning of the meeting.
- **No more than 3 talking points.** If you bring five, you cover none of them.
- **One ask, always.** The QBR where you walk out without asking for anything specific was a social call.
- **Flag problems before they flag them.** If fill rate dropped, own it on the one-pager — don't wait for the contractor to bring it up.
- **Save to `WORK AREAS/Customer-Accounts/[account]-qbr-project/outputs/`** with `[Account]_QBR-One-Pager_[quarter].md`.

---

## Follow-up email pattern

Within 24 hours of the QBR, the rep sends a follow-up confirming the decisions. See Format 1 in `email-formats-template.md` and the filled-in example in the Acme Plumbing QBR example project.

The follow-up is for closing loops, not opening new ones. Don't pitch anything in the 24-hour follow-up — do that on the next call.
