## How to use this

**Before a vendor QBR:** Say "draft the vendor scorecard for [vendor] for [period]." Paste the ERP purchase detail, the fill-rate dashboard, and the rebate tracker. Claude fills in the data sections. You add the market feedback and the grade.

**For an annual carry-line review:** Generate scorecards for all primary vendors in a category and compare side-by-side. This is how you make an informed "keep / drop / replace" call.

**For the annual planning cycle:** Use the current scorecard as the baseline for next year's commitments both ways.

---

## Notes

- **Every number comes from a source.** Claude won't invent fill rates or rebate $ from memory. Paste the source reports.
- **The "commitments" sections are where QBRs prove their worth.** If you don't track what each side promised, the meeting is ceremonial. If you do, it compounds.
- **Grade with discipline.** An "A" vendor every quarter means you're not grading honestly. Most vendors are "B" or "C" — the question is which direction they're trending.
- **Save scorecards to `WORK AREAS/Procurement-Vendors/[vendor]-qbr-project/outputs/`** with the naming convention `[Vendor]_Scorecard_[period].md`. Year-over-year comparison is invaluable.
