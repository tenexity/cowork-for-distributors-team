# Project brief: Top-25 account QBR cycle — Q2 2026

> This is an example project showing what a filled-in brief looks like for a real distributor workflow. Delete this folder once you've seen the pattern, or keep it as a reference. Your real projects go in their own `-project` folders alongside this one within the Customer-Accounts work area.

## What is this project?

Running Quarterly Business Reviews with our top 25 accounts for Q2 2026. Each QBR reviews carry performance, fill rate, rebate-eligible programs they're on, outstanding back-orders, and spring HVAC season readiness. We're behind on this — the cycle was supposed to start in early April and we're already mid-month. Need to catch up without cutting corners.

Spring is peak season for 18 of our top 25 (HVAC-heavy plumbers, mechanical contractors, service companies), so the Q2 QBR is the single most important one of the year for most of them. If we miss these, we lose the chance to influence their spring purchasing and risk them drifting to competitors.

## Goal

Complete all 25 QBRs by June 15. Each QBR delivers a branded one-page summary to the contractor the morning of the meeting and a follow-up action email within 24 hours.

**Success criteria:**
1. All 25 QBRs held by June 15 (6 weeks from project start)
2. Every meeting has a one-page summary delivered before the call
3. Every meeting produces a follow-up email with three specific action items within 24 hours
4. Minimum 3 account expansions identified across the cycle (new carry line, new branch coverage, new project quote)
5. Zero "we didn't know they were unhappy" surprises — if an account is at risk, we find out during QBR, not at renewal

## Audience

The contractors at our top 25 accounts. Mix of owner-operators (small plumbing companies), service managers at mid-size mechanical firms, and purchasing managers at larger multi-branch service companies.

They're busy, they're skeptical of "meetings for the sake of meetings," and they will tell us immediately if the QBR is a waste of their time. Every QBR needs to be worth their 30-45 minutes — which means data they haven't seen, decisions we need their input on, and a clear ask.

## Key constraints

- **Timing:** 6 weeks. Roughly 4-5 QBRs per week depending on rep availability.
- **Data freshness:** Every QBR needs ERP data from the week of the meeting, not a month-old pull.
- **Rep bandwidth:** 3 outside reps covering the top 25. They can't do 5 QBRs in a week on top of normal territory calls.
- **No surprises policy:** If a QBR is going to surface a credit or margin issue, Finance and Procurement need a heads-up before the meeting.
- **Branded output:** One-page summary needs company letterhead. Follow-up email from rep's personal signature.
