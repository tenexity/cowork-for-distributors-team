# My voice — individual override

This file is **optional**. By default, every employee at this company writes in the company voice defined in `ABOUT ME/COMPANY/voice-and-writing-rules.md`.

Use this file only if you have a distinctive personal style that's worth preserving on top of the company voice — for example, a long-tenured outside sales rep with a specific phrasing customers know him for, or a branch manager whose team is used to a particular tone.

Anything you specify here **overrides** the company voice for your writing. Anything you leave blank uses the company default.

The banned-words and structural-pattern rules in `voice-and-writing-rules.md` are **non-overridable** — they're company hard-floor rules, not preferences.

## How this layers with COMPANY/voice-and-writing-rules.md

Where this file specifies a tone, characteristic phrase, structural default, or banned word — it wins over the company default for *your* writing.

Where this file is blank or silent on a topic — the company default applies.

The company-wide hard-floor rules (banned AI words, no-invented-numbers, formatting standards, the five-point test) **apply to everyone, always**. They're not overridable by anything in this file.

For most employees, this file stays mostly blank. The company voice is good enough for 90% of communication. Override only where your personal style genuinely differs and matters.
